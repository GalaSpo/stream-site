What the app is

GalaSpo Stream is a free iOS app that lets you broadcast live video from an iPhone directly to your own YouTube channel, with a scoreboard overlay (teams, colors, logos, score, and game clock) burned into the video. It's built for junior athlete parents, coaches, and volunteers, and works equally well for adult leagues.

Your YouTube account and the YouTube API

GalaSpo Stream connects to your YouTube channel through Google's own sign-in so it can create, schedule, and manage the live broadcasts you start from the app. This use of the YouTube API Services is subject to the YouTube Terms of Service, and by using GalaSpo Stream's YouTube features you also agree to the Google Privacy Policy. You can revoke GalaSpo Stream's access to your account at any time from your Google Account permissions page.

Your video stream is sent directly to YouTube — GalaSpo does not operate a relay server the broadcast passes through, and does not separately record or store a copy of it. See the Privacy Policy for the full detail on what the app accesses and stores.

YouTube's own age & live-streaming rules

Separately from these terms, YouTube sets its own rules for who can stream and what can be shown — GalaSpo Stream doesn't set or enforce these, and has no control over YouTube's moderation or takedown decisions. As of this writing, YouTube requires a streamer to be at least 16 to live stream solo; streamers aged 13–15 must be visibly accompanied by an adult. These age thresholds, and other content rules, can also vary by country based on local law. See YouTube's live streaming restrictions and child safety policy pages for the current, authoritative rules for your country, since YouTube can change them independently of this app.

When an adult operates GalaSpo Stream to broadcast a youth game, that's treated by YouTube as adult-run coverage of a public sporting event — not a minor hosting their own stream. A few practical things that help keep a broadcast like that trouble-free:

  • Don't mark the broadcast "Made for Kids" if you want live chat available — that setting turns chat off entirely.
  • In YouTube Studio, turn on "Hold potentially inappropriate messages for review," and consider having someone moderate chat during the stream.
  • Give the broadcast a clear title (team names, age group, tournament) so it's obviously an organized sporting event.
  • Avoid close, personal camera work or narration that reveals a child's full name, school, or routine.
